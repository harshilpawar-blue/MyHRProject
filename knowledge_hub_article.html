5 Resume Mistakes Senior Engineers Make (And How to Fix Them)

Rohan Mehta

Senior Technical Recruiter

Nov 24, 2024
6 min read

You have 8 years of Java experience, you've led teams, and you've shipped scalable code. Yet, you're getting ghosted by recruiters. The problem isn't your codeâ€”it's your documentation.

1. Listing "Responsibilities" Instead of "Impact"

Most engineering resumes look like a job description. "Responsible for writing Java code" tells me nothing. "Reduced API latency by 40% by refactoring the caching layer" tells me you are an asset.

The Fix: Use the X-Y-Z Formula

"Accomplished [X] as measured by [Y], by doing [Z]."

2. The "Keyword Stuffing" Trap

Listing 50 technologies in a skills block looks desperate. If you list Kubernetes, be prepared to answer deep architectural questions about it.

3. Ignoring the ATS (Applicant Tracking System)

Fancy two-column layouts with graphics often get garbled by older ATS parsers used by Indian MNCs. Keep the formatting clean and hierarchical.

Quick Checklist for your Resume:

  • Remove photo (unless requested specifically).
  • Convert generic objectives to a "Professional Summary".
  • Ensure hyperlinks to GitHub/LinkedIn actually work.
  • Save as PDF, not Docx, to preserve formatting.
